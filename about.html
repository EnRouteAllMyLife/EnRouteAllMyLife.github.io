Total Hip Arthroplasty on Pelvic Mobility

This is a prospective cohort study focusing on the impacts of total hip arthroplasty on pelvic mobility. The study centers are The First Hospital of Jilin University and The Sixth Hospital of Xinjiang Medical University. The primary objectives of this study are threefold:

  • To study changes of pelvic mobility after hip arthroplasty and associated influencing factors in patients with different diagnosis.

  • To establish a prediction model for pelvic stiffness remission after hip replacement in patients with DDH.

  • To establish a method to screen patients with possible abnormal pelvic mobility based on routine preoperative examinations.

Coronal Plane Alignment of the Knee Classification

This is a retrospective cohort study focusing on the distribution of coronal plane alignment of the knee (CPAK) classification among the osteoarthritis and the healthy population. Particularly focusing on Chinese population, the primary objectives of this study are threefold:

  • To investigate the distribution of CPAK within the Chinese population; to determine if it aligns with previous study.

  • To study the impact of OA progression on CPAK classification changes

  • To establish a machine learning algorithm for predicting the phenotypic structure of osteoarthritic knee joints.
